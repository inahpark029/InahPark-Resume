Medical Scribe for Joshua Clinic- Scribed for family medicine and pediatrics with Dr. Banson and Dr. Chi. Working alongside Dr. Banson, I assisted with charts, learned procedure methods, and increased my knowledge of human physiology. In pediatrics with Dr. Chi, I performed routine check-ups, discussed with patients, and was taught the differences between adolescent anatomy versus post pubescent-- both the chemical make-up and physical aspects. Not only have I worked at a medical clinic, but also joined the physicians to a connected hospital to explore other specialties such as neurology, cardiology, oncology, and orthopedics. While they performed surgeries, I shadowed nurses and physicians from ER floors to PICU. Intern Paramedic and EMT Training- Entered an EMT training program to pursue a career in the medical field as a paramedic, surgeon, or ER physician. Received training to be able to read EKG machines, insert endotracheal intubation, vein access, assess situations, damage control, and CPR. Reading the EKG machine was the most challenging when identifying the differences between V-fib, V-tach, sinus tach, and sinus brady rhythms. Having knowledge of damage control and improving assessing situations prepared me for unexpected cases away from EMT training. Training alongside my colleagues, we performed team exercises to stay grounded under pressure and flexibility with unknown surroundings. I was able to have hands-on experience in an ambulance and follow the paramedics to respond to a few emergency calls. During the emergency calls, I executed the procedures however falter a couple times which taught determination and resistance. Through this training and interning, I learned to have mindful thinking under pressure, stability while executing my emergency knowledge, and adaptability to different environments.

Assisting Wendy Ro and other researchers in this study shaped my leadership and developed my creative thinking for future experminations.
